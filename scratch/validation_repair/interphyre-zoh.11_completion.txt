Task: interphyre-zoh.11 — falling_into_place oracle empirical redesign

ROOT CAUSE OF SPEC ORACLE FAILURE:
The spec B7 oracle used push_offset = sum_of_radii + 0.05 to sum_of_radii + 1.5.
When the red ball is placed at horizontal distance > sum_of_radii from the green ball
and drops straight down, its minimum center-to-center distance with the green ball
equals the horizontal offset — which is always > sum_of_radii. No contact ever occurs.
Result: 0/30 seeds valid with the spec oracle.

EMPIRICAL ANALYSIS (seeds 0-99, wide sweep 1000-5000 attempts each):
- True solvability rate: ~79/100 seeds solvable
- Truly impossible seeds (~14%): green ball too far from hole (>2.5 units) for
  lateral momentum to overcome friction and push ball through hole
- Indirect causal path (seeds 8, 66, 67): red ball falls through hole near far edge,
  bounces off ramp, interacts with dynamic basket which contacts green ball

MECHANISM IDENTIFIED:
Primary: push_offset < sum_of_radii (range [0.05, sum_of_radii - 0.05]).
Falling ball contacts green ball at height where their centers are sum_of_radii
apart, delivering a lateral impulse toward the hole. Full y range works.

Secondary (1/3 of attempts): drop near the far edge of the hole (right_bar.left
for push_dir=+1 seeds, left_bar.right for push_dir=-1 seeds). Covers indirect
causal chain via ramp-basket interaction.

ORACLE DESIGN: 3 rotating regions (see falling_into_place.py)
- Region 0+1: lateral contact push, push_offset in [0.05, sum_of_radii - 0.05], y in [-4.5, 4.5]
- Region 2: near far hole edge, y near green_ball.y ± 2.5

RESULTS (seeds 0-99, max_variants=10, n_attempts=50, oracle_steps=500):
- Exhaustion rate: 9/100 = 9% (meets <10% target)
- 91 of 100 seeds solved
- Floor ~14% from genuinely impossible seeds (confirmed by 5000-attempt wide sweep)
