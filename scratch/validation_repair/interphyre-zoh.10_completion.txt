ROOT CAUSE OF SPEC ORACLE FAILURE:
The spec B6 oracle placed the red ball at push_offset = sum_of_radii + 0.05 to +1.5 away
from the green ball on the far side. Since the green ball starts UNSUPPORTED (floating in
air) and both balls begin at rest, they fall at identical gravitational rates — no contact
ever occurs. Result: 0/30 seeds valid.

CRITICAL GEOMETRIC INSIGHT:
The overlap approach (push_offset < sum_of_radii) from other oracle fixes (the_cradle,
falling_into_place) does NOT work for keyhole. Box2D resolves large overlaps through POSITION
CORRECTION, not velocity impulses, when both bodies start at rest. The green ball's position
jumps rightward but v_x stays 0. The ball then falls straight down onto the LEFT floor
(black_pad), never reaching the keyhole gap.

EMPIRICALLY DISCOVERED MECHANISM (floor-bounce):
1. Place red ball BELOW the green ball, to the far side from x=0 (small horizontal offset)
2. Both balls fall under gravity; the red ball (starting lower) hits the FLOOR FIRST
3. Red ball bounces upward and contacts the still-falling green ball at a glancing angle
4. The small horizontal offset creates a diagonal contact normal → horizontal impulse component
5. Green ball gains lateral velocity toward x=0 and navigates through the keyhole gap
6. Ball lands on purple_pad on the far side → sustained contact → success
